The origin of a crude punnchline in the 2008 film Tropic Thunder, I am Sam is the epitome of an oscar-bait film that attempts to draw attention to marginalized groups, but the film itself is unwatchable. Even beyond the casting of Sean Penn as an intellectually disabled man named Sam Dawson, the "shakycam" cinematography and uninteresting courtroom drama left me bored ater about 5 minutes. The story follows Sam as he attempts to win back custody of his daughter Lucy (Dakota Fanning) with the help of attorney Rita Harrison (Michele Pfeiffer). How did he get his daughter? By sleeping with a homeless woman who abandoned them both because she didn't want a child. How did this come about? The film doesn't tell the audience and, frankly, I'm going to assume criminal action from one party. Aside from Dakota Fanning's performanec, nothing about this is redeemable.

2/10
