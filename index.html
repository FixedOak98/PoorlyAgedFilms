What is a Poorly Aged Film?

The United Church of Lesser Quality Films is dedicated to the evaluation and praise/ridicule of films that have failed to withstand the test of time. While the focus is primarily on films that have aged poorly in very short period after release (within 10 years or so), there will occasionally be reviews covering classic films that have may have aged poorly over a longer period of time. Now, you may be asking, "What makes a film poorly aged?". It's a fair question given that we live an era where things can age very quickly due to the advent of the internet. This also has exascerbated our tendency to call out statements, actions and works that have been proven wrong. That being said, there is a set of criteria that we follow here at The United Church of Lesser Quality Films. Here is some the criteria that we follow in determining whether a film has failed to stand the test of time:
Special Effects
Special effects have come a long way in film. There will inevitably be films with effects that were fine, even revolutionary, for the time that fail to capture audiences in the same way. What we are looking to evaluate are films whose effects were either extremely poor even for the time, or were quickly superceded by films that revolutionized special effects in the same decade.
Casting choices
This can range from an actor playing someone of a completely inappropriate age to... decisions that are made solely to put a big name in role that they otherwise shouldn't be playing. Whether it be racism, nepotism, ableism, or any other form of discrimination, there have been numerous instances of this practice in films and it will continue in different forms in the future.
Outdated plot points
Many stories or plot points that have been placed in movies that seemed benign at the time before eventually being coming to be seen as problemtic. An example that is covered multiple times on this website is how people perceived members of the LGBTQ+ community in film in ways that boggle our minds.
Off-camera drama and things outside the control of the filmmakers
The Isle of Dr. Moreau (1996), in which numerous off-screen issues inflated the budget and turned the cast and directors against each other.
Events mirroring Real-life
See: Luc Besson, director of Leon the Professional (1994) as an example
