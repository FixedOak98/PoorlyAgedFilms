One of the easiest one out of ten ratings I've ever given! The circumstances surrounding the making of Tiptoes were cursed from the beginning, even down to the able-bodied Gary Oldman and his decision to play Rolph, a man with dwarfism. Watching Olman as he attempts to pull off prtraying a lttle person amongst a sea of actors who do have dwarfism only emphasizes how unconvincing are jarring the casting choice is. Film critic Nathan Rabbin went so far as to compare the casting to blackface at the time. As far as the actual story goes, it follows fiances Steven (Matthew McConaughy) and Carol (Kate Beckinsale) discover that they are expecting as Carol learns that Steven has been hiding his family from her. Steven is the only person in his family without dwarfism, a fact Kate discovers by meeting Steve's borther Rolph (Oldman). This creates conflict between the couple as both have health concerns over their unborn child, a justifiable concern that is undercut by Beckinsale's poor acting and some truly odd dialogue between the two.

Meanwhile they are interupted by the romantic escapades of Rolph's friend Maurice (Peter Dinklage); a French litle person with extremely Marxist attitudes, and Lucy (Patricia Arquette), a free-spirited vagabond. These two are completely unecessary addtions to the film with awkward dialogue headlined by Lucy responding to a question of where she was from with the response "Well, my body is orignally from New York...".

All of this is bad enough, and it doesn't do anything to distract from the fact that Gary Oldman is on his knees the entire time. Their attempts to hide Oldman's legs are almost comical in how they thought this was a sound decision. Whether it's dgitally editing out him from the knee down to constantly shooting Oldman above the waist. But the cherry on top is when Oldman is waist deep in a couch in order to hide his real body while stuffen legs are protruding from the couch. It almost makes Oldman look like a muppet.

Things, understandably, did not go well off-screen either. Director Matthew Bright was fired after production was completed, while its intial screening at the Sundance was quickly retracted. Overall, this movie was a overly-melodramatic, boring or creepy when it wanted to be sweet, and often had me watching with my hands in my face.

1/10
